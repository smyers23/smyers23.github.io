California Science Policy Fellow

Originally Posted: June 29, 2026

Since finishing my PhD, I have been working as a science policy fellow in California with the California Council on Science & Technology. Like the program I worked to establish in Arizona,, this fellowship aims to take scientists and engineers and place them in government to serve as policy advisors. I was very fortunate to be placed in the California Senate Committee on Transportation where I have been doing the work of a policy consultant.

In this role I have had the opportunity to play a direct role in the policy process. I have analyzed legislation, negotiated bills, made vote recommendations to legislators, and prepared talking points and research documents to brief our committee chair. I’ve also helped organize two different informational hearings: one on autonomous vehicle technology and one on the California High-Speed Rail Project.

So far I have worked on a wide array of topics, including autonomous vehicles; short-line railroads; DUI; electric aircraft; delivery robots; transit systems; state highways; commuter, regional, and high-speed rail; and more.
